Searching

Starling supports searching for songs based on keywords. By default, keywords are looked for in a track's title, album, artist, genre and source fields. If a keyword is a substring of any of those fields, the track is considered to match. If multiple keywords are given, then a track must match all keywords. It is possible to search for a specific field by prefixing the keyword with the field of interest followed by a colon. For instance, to find tracks by the BBC, one could use artist:bbc.

Keywords can be joined together using or. For instance, searching for ambient or electronic will find tracks that have the string ambient or the string electronic in their respective title, album, artist, genre or source field. Likewise, and, not and parenthesis can be used.

It is also possible to match against some properties of a track. Starling currently supports four properties:

  • added - the amount of time since the track was added to the data base,
  • played - the amount of time since the track was last played,
  • play-count - the number of times a song has been played, and,
  • rating - the songs rating.

To search for a property, suffix the property with a colon, followed by a comparison operator, either <, >, <=, >=, =, or !=. Time is measured in seconds, however, m, h, d, W, M, and Y, can be used to multiple by the number of seconds in a minute, hour, day, week, month (30 days) or year, respectively.

For instance, to search for songs added in the last week that have not yet been played, one could use:

added:<1W and play-count:=0

For 4 and 5 star songs that have not been played in the last 3 days:

rating:>4 and last-played:>3D
